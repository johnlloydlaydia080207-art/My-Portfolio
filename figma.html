Design Improvements — Explanation

Visual Identity & Color System: The mid-fidelity wireframe used only grey placeholder boxes and flat layouts. In the high-fidelity version, a warm, editorial color palette was introduced — deep brown (#3B1F0E), rich amber (#C8702A), and cream (#FAF7F2) — to give BookWorm a premium, literary feel that resonates with book lovers. Typography was elevated by pairing Playfair Display (a serif with editorial character) for headings and DM Sans (clean, modern) for body text, creating a clear visual hierarchy. Real iconography, gradient book covers, and layered card shadows replaced the grey boxes, making each screen feel like a finished, polished product rather than a sketch.

Usability & Interaction Design: Several structural improvements were made to follow usability principles. The Home screen now features a personalized greeting, a prominent search bar with a filter icon, horizontally scrollable featured cards, and category chips for quick filtering — reducing the cognitive load of browsing. The Book Detail screen consolidates all purchase-decision information (cover, price, rating, description, reviews) in one scrollable view, with clear primary ("Buy Now") and secondary ("Wishlist") CTAs. The Cart uses quantity controls, a subtotal summary, and a visually dominant checkout button to guide users toward conversion. The Profile screen adds a stats row (Books, Wishlist, Reviews) for engagement and groups menu items clearly with color-coded icons, improving scannability. A persistent bottom navigation bar across all main screens ensures consistent wayfinding throughout the app.
